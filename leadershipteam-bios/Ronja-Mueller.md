<p>
  Ronja Mueller is a postdoctoral researcher in cognitive psychology at the ICAN Institute for Cognitive and Affective Neuroscience (ICAN) 
  and the Institute for Forensic Psychology and Medicine (IFPM), both at the Medical School Hamburg, Germany. 
  Ronja obtained her PhD in Psychology at the University of Bamberg and completed a DAAD funded research stay in Stirling, Scotland.
  Her research interests include the study of face recognition and storage mechanisms, with special attention to the flexibility of 
  face representations and the information that is stored in face memory. She further analyzes factors influencing face identification in police context.
